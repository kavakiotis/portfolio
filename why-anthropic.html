Why Anthropic

Mission alignment, proven expertise, and a vision for responsible AI commercialization

The Mission: Safe, Beneficial AI

Throughout my career, I've been guided by what I call a "Force for Good" philosophy - the belief that business success and positive impact aren't opposing forces, but reinforcing ones. This is why Anthropic's mission resonates so deeply with me.

Anthropic isn't just building AI - you're building it with interpretability, safety, and human benefit at the core. In a landscape where many companies treat AI safety as a constraint to optimize around, Anthropic treats it as a foundational design principle. The work on Constitutional AI, the focus on making Claude's reasoning transparent, the commitment to building systems that humans can actually trust - these aren't marketing talking points. They're architectural choices that shape every output.

As someone who has spent years selling enterprise technology, I know how important trust is. Healthcare buyers don't adopt AI unless they can understand it. Manufacturing engineers don't rely on tools they can't verify. Anthropic's approach to interpretability and safety isn't just ethically sound - it's commercially strategic. It's what makes enterprise adoption possible.

Values Alignment

My personal philosophy - "Doing the Right Thing" and being a "Force for Good" - aligns directly with Anthropic's public benefit corporation status. This isn't coincidental. I specifically seek organizations where commercial success and positive impact reinforce each other. That alignment is rare, and it's why Anthropic stands out.

My AI Journey

I'm not new to AI transformation. As Global Head of Customer Success at Siemens, I pioneered an AI-powered sales and customer success transformation that delivered measurable results: 40% reduction in sales cycles, 35% increase in conversion rates. I did this by integrating AI tools into daily workflows - using intent data, automated engagement, and AI assistants for research synthesis and content generation.

This wasn't theoretical. I built frameworks for AI-assisted proposal development, customer communications, and research synthesis. I learned what works in enterprise AI adoption and what doesn't. I understand both the potential and the limitations. Most importantly, I understand that AI adoption is fundamentally a change management challenge, not a technology challenge.

This hands-on experience with AI in enterprise GTM contexts is directly applicable to Anthropic's challenge: scaling Claude adoption across industry verticals while maintaining the trust and safety that differentiate Anthropic from competitors.

Industry Expertise Where It Matters

Anthropic is targeting key enterprise verticals including Healthcare, Financial Services, Technology, and Manufacturing. I bring deep expertise in two of these - Healthcare and Manufacturing - built over 15+ years of selling complex technology solutions to these sectors.

I understand the Healthcare buyer: the CMIO concerned about clinical validity, the CIO worried about integration, the compliance officer focused on HIPAA. I know why healthcare sales cycles take 6-18 months and how to navigate them. I've sold to NHS Trusts, hospitals, and clinical facilities.

I understand the Manufacturing buyer: the COO obsessed with operational efficiency, the quality leader managing ISO compliance, the digital transformation lead trying to modernize legacy systems. I've sold building automation and IoT solutions to industrial facilities across EMEA and APAC.

This isn't generic "enterprise sales experience." It's specific, vertical expertise in the exact markets Anthropic is targeting.

What I Would Build

If I join Anthropic as GTM Strategy & Operations Partner, I would focus on building the frameworks that enable scalable enterprise adoption. The Healthcare GTM Playbook and Manufacturing GTM Playbook on this site demonstrate this approach.

These aren't theoretical exercises - they're the kind of industry-specific frameworks I would create for Anthropic's sales teams. Buyer personas with specific pain points and value messaging. Sales motions tailored to industry compliance requirements and procurement processes. Objection handling based on real enterprise concerns. Success metrics that matter to industry buyers.

I built a Partner Playbook at Siemens that generated $24M in pipeline. I would bring that same methodology to building industry playbooks for Anthropic's verticals.

The Meta-Point

This website itself demonstrates my approach. I used Claude to help develop these industry GTM frameworks - showing that I understand Anthropic's product, that I can collaborate effectively with AI, and that I'm already thinking about how to position Claude for enterprise buyers. The playbooks aren't just portfolio pieces; they're proof of concept for the work I would do at Anthropic.

The Bigger Picture

We're at an inflection point. Enterprise AI adoption is accelerating, but it's also becoming more scrutinized. The companies that win won't be those with the most powerful models - they'll be those that earn enterprise trust. Anthropic is positioned to be that company, but realizing this potential requires GTM strategies that translate technical excellence into commercial success.

I want to be part of building that. Not because it's a good career move (though it is), but because I believe responsible AI commercialization matters. How AI gets sold shapes how it gets used. The frameworks, playbooks, and sales motions we build will influence how enterprises adopt AI - safely or recklessly, thoughtfully or carelessly.

That's work worth doing. That's why Anthropic.
